# Trauma

Spinal trauma encompasses a wide spectrum of injuries ranging from stable compression fractures to devastating fracture-dislocations with spinal cord injury. Radiologists play a critical role in the rapid and accurate identification of spinal injuries, classification of fracture patterns, assessment of stability, and detection of associated soft tissue and neurological injuries.

This section covers the major classification systems used in spine trauma (AO Spine, Denis, TLICS, SLIC), clinical decision rules for imaging (NEXUS, Canadian C-Spine Rule), and individual fracture types organized by anatomical region — from the craniocervical junction through the sacrum and coccyx. Soft tissue injuries including spinal cord injury, epidural hematoma, and traumatic disc herniation are also addressed.
