# Modic Endplate Changes

## Definition

Modic changes are MRI signal alterations in the vertebral body marrow adjacent to degenerating intervertebral discs. Described by Modic et al. in 1988, they are classified into three types based on their T1 and T2 signal characteristics and represent different stages of the endplate degenerative process.

## Classification

| Type | T1 Signal | T2 Signal | STIR | Pathology | Clinical Correlation |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Type 1** | Low (dark) | High (bright) | Bright | Vascularized fibrous tissue, inflammatory edema | Most strongly associated with active low back pain |
| **Type 2** | High (bright) | Iso to slightly high | Variable | Yellow (fatty) marrow replacement | Stable, chronic; less symptomatic |
| **Type 3** | Low (dark) | Low (dark) | Dark | Subchondral sclerosis | End-stage; dense woven bone |

### Mixed Types

- Types can coexist at the same level (e.g., Type 1/2 mixed)
- Types can convert over time: Type 1 → Type 2 → Type 3 (progressive stabilization)
- Type 2 can occasionally revert to Type 1 (reactivation of inflammation)

## Imaging Findings

### MRI — The Definitive Modality

- Changes are best seen on **sagittal T1 and T2** sequences
- STIR is useful for confirming Type 1 (edema-like) changes
- Changes parallel the endplate and extend into the adjacent marrow — typically symmetric above and below the disc
- Most common at **L4–L5 and L5–S1**

### Key Differential Considerations

| Modic Type 1 vs. | Distinguishing Feature |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Discitis/osteomyelitis** | Modic 1 preserves the disc signal and endplate cortex; infection destroys the disc and erodes the endplate |
| **Metastasis** | Modic changes are endplate-centered and bilateral; metastases are typically more focal and eccentric |
| **Acute fracture edema** | Fracture shows vertebral height loss and cortical disruption |

!!! tip "Clinical Pearl"
    **Modic Type 1 changes are the most clinically significant** — they correlate with active inflammation and are the type most strongly associated with low back pain. Some studies suggest that patients with Modic 1 changes may benefit from anti-inflammatory treatment or, in refractory cases, antibiotic therapy (the controversial Modic Antibiotic Spine Therapy — MAST protocol), based on the theory that low-grade bacterial infection of the disc contributes to Modic 1 changes. This remains a topic of active research and debate.

## Key Points

- Modic changes are classified into three types based on T1/T2 signal at the vertebral endplates
- Type 1 (edema/inflammation) is most symptomatic; Type 2 (fatty) is stable; Type 3 (sclerosis) is end-stage
- Types can convert over time, generally progressing from 1 → 2 → 3
- Most common at L4–L5 and L5–S1
- Type 1 must be distinguished from infection — endplate integrity and disc signal are key differentiators
- Sagittal T1 and T2 MRI are essential for classification
