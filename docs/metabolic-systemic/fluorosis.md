# Fluorosis

## Overview

Chronic excessive fluoride intake causes diffuse osteosclerosis and ligamentous calcification. In the spine, it produces diffuse dense sclerosis of the vertebral bodies, calcification of spinal ligaments (ALL, PLL, ligamentum flavum), and spinal canal narrowing. Endemic in areas with high fluoride in drinking water (India, China).

## Key Imaging Distinction

Fluorosis calcifies ligaments AND produces dense vertebral sclerosis. DISH calcifies the ALL but does NOT cause vertebral body sclerosis. AS produces syndesmophytes, not ligament calcification. Fluorosis is denser and more uniform than either.

!!! tip "Clinical Pearl"
    Fluorosis, DISH, and AS can all produce a stiff, ankylosed spine. The key distinctions: fluorosis shows **dense vertebral body sclerosis + ligament calcification**; DISH shows **flowing anterior ossification with normal bone density**; AS shows **thin marginal syndesmophytes with sacroiliac fusion**.

## Key Points

- Diffuse vertebral sclerosis with ligament calcification
- Endemic in high-fluoride water regions
- Spinal canal narrowing from ligament ossification can cause myelopathy
- Distinguish from DISH (no vertebral sclerosis) and AS (syndesmophytes, sacroiliac fusion)
