# Bamboo Spine

## Definition

Bamboo spine is the end-stage radiographic appearance of ankylosing spondylitis, characterized by complete fusion of the vertebral column through bridging syndesmophytes, facet joint ankylosis, posterior ligament ossification, and interspinous ligament calcification. The term derives from the resemblance of the fused spine to a stalk of bamboo on frontal radiograph.

## Imaging Findings

### Radiography
- **AP view** — The "trolley track" sign: three vertical radiodense lines representing the fused spinous processes (central line) and the fused facet joints/syndesmophytes (lateral lines)
- **Lateral view** — Continuous vertical ossification along the anterior and posterior margins of the vertebral bodies (syndesmophytes), squared vertebral bodies, and fused disc spaces
- **"Dagger sign"** — A single central radiodense line on AP view from ossification of the interspinous and supraspinous ligaments

### CT
- Continuous bridging bone connecting all vertebral bodies
- Complete facet joint ankylosis
- Posterior element fusion
- Ossification of the interspinous ligaments

### MRI
- Low T1 and T2 signal in the fused syndesmophytes and posterior elements (mature bone)
- Fatty marrow replacement in the fused vertebral corners (chronic "healed" Romanus lesions — bright on T1)
- STIR may show areas of active inflammation even in an otherwise fused spine

!!! tip "Clinical Pearl"
    The **"trolley track" sign** on AP radiograph — three vertical radiodense lines — is pathognomonic of bamboo spine in AS. It must be distinguished from DISH, which produces flowing anterior ossification but preserves the sacroiliac joints and facet joints. In AS, the sacroiliac joints are fused; in DISH, they are normal.

## Fracture Risk

The fused bamboo spine is extremely vulnerable to fracture:

- Fractures behave like **long bone fractures** — they are inherently unstable because the fused spine has no mobile segments to absorb energy
- Most common at the **cervicothoracic junction** (C5–T1)
- All three columns fracture simultaneously, often with significant displacement
- **Epidural hematoma** is common and can cause rapid neurological deterioration
- Even minor trauma (ground-level falls) can cause devastating fracture-dislocations

## Key Points

- Bamboo spine is end-stage ankylosing spondylitis with complete spinal fusion
- "Trolley track" sign on AP radiograph and "dagger sign" are characteristic
- Distinguish from DISH by sacroiliac joint fusion (AS) vs preservation (DISH)
- The fused spine is extremely brittle — fractures are unstable and often devastating
- CT and MRI of the entire spine are essential after even minor trauma in AS patients
