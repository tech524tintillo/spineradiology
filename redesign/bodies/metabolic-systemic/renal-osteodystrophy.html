Renal Osteodystrophy¶

Definition¶

Renal osteodystrophy encompasses the skeletal manifestations of chronic kidney disease, including secondary hyperparathyroidism, osteomalacia, osteosclerosis, and amyloid deposition. In the spine, the most characteristic finding is the "rugger jersey" spine.

Imaging Findings¶

Radiography/CT¶

  • "Rugger jersey" spine — Alternating bands of sclerosis and lucency within the vertebral bodies on lateral radiograph, resembling the horizontal stripes of a rugby jersey. The sclerotic bands are at the endplates (subchondral sclerosis) with a lucent central zone.
  • Diffuse osteosclerosis — Generalized increased bone density
  • Brown tumors — Lytic lesions from secondary hyperparathyroidism (rare in the spine)
  • Soft tissue calcifications — Vascular and periarticular calcifications

MRI¶

  • The sclerotic endplate bands appear as low T1 and T2 signal
  • The lucent central zone has relatively normal marrow signal

Clinical Pearl

The "rugger jersey" spine — horizontal bands of dense endplate sclerosis alternating with lucent central vertebral body zones — is virtually pathognomonic of renal osteodystrophy. It results from secondary hyperparathyroidism causing subchondral bone resorption followed by reactive sclerosis. When you see this pattern, check the patient's renal function.

Key Points¶

  • "Rugger jersey" spine is the characteristic finding — alternating sclerotic and lucent bands
  • Results from secondary hyperparathyroidism in chronic kidney disease
  • Diffuse osteosclerosis and soft tissue calcifications are additional findings
  • Brown tumors are rare in the spine but can occur
